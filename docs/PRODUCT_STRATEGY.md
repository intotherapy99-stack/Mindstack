# MindStack — Product Strategy

## 1. Vision

**3-Year Vision:** Become the default operating system for mental health practice in India — the tool every therapist opens first thing in the morning.

**10-Year Vision:** Power the infrastructure layer for mental health delivery across South and Southeast Asia, enabling not just individual practitioners but clinics, hospitals, NGOs, and government programs.

---

## 2. Strategic Positioning

### The Wedge: Supervision Tracking

Every mental health platform globally competes on scheduling and notes. MindStack enters the market through a **uniquely Indian wedge: supervision hour tracking**.

India's mental health regulatory landscape is evolving. The Rehabilitation Council of India (RCI) and emerging professional bodies increasingly require documented supervision hours for licensure and continued practice. Yet no tool tracks this. Practitioners use Excel sheets and WhatsApp screenshots.

MindStack makes supervision tracking effortless — and in doing so, becomes the system of record for a practitioner's professional development. Once we own that relationship, expanding into full practice management is natural.

**Why this wedge works:**
- Zero competition (no one tracks Indian supervision hours)
- Regulatory tailwind (requirements are tightening, not loosening)
- Network effects (supervisors invite supervisees, and vice versa)
- High switching cost (supervision logs are career-critical; you don't migrate them)

### Positioning Statement

> For mental health professionals in India who manage their practice across disconnected tools, MindStack is the only practice management platform that combines client management, clinical documentation, payments, and supervision tracking in a single workspace — purpose-built for Indian regulatory requirements, payment patterns, and professional culture.

## 3. Defensibility Moat

### Layer 1: Data Network Effects (Months 0-12)
- Supervision sessions create two-sided relationships (supervisor + supervisee)
- Each relationship generates recurring sessions, increasing switching cost
- Referral network connects practitioners, making the platform more valuable with each new user

### Layer 2: Workflow Lock-In (Months 6-18)
- Clinical notes accumulate over time — practitioners won't migrate years of SOAP notes
- Payment history creates the financial record of the practice
- Client relationships and session history become the practitioner's institutional memory

### Layer 3: Platform Effects (Months 12-36)
- Client-facing booking creates demand-side value (clients find therapists through MindStack profiles)
- Community spaces become the place where Indian MH professionals discuss cases, share referrals, and learn
- API integrations with Indian-specific tools (Razorpay, Tally for accounting, WhatsApp Business)

### Layer 4: Regulatory Moat (Months 18+)
- First mover on DPDP Act compliance features
- Pre-built templates for RCI supervision documentation
- Potential for official recognition by professional bodies as an approved tracking system

## 4. Growth Strategy

### Phase 1: Supply-Side Seeding (Months 0-6)
**Goal:** 500 registered practitioners

- Target early-career therapists and trainees (highest pain around supervision tracking)
- Partner with 3-5 training institutes in Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore
- Free tier with generous limits (up to 5 clients)
- Launch in WhatsApp groups where Indian MH professionals already congregate

### Phase 2: Demand-Side Activation (Months 6-12)
**Goal:** 2,000 practitioners, 500 active clients booking through MindStack

- Enable public practitioner profiles with SEO optimization (therapist in Mumbai, psychologist in Delhi)
- Client self-serve booking reduces practitioner admin burden
- Each practitioner's profile becomes a landing page — organic acquisition

### Phase 3: Community & Network (Months 12-18)
**Goal:** MindStack becomes the daily-open tool

- Peer referral network drives inter-practitioner engagement
- Community spaces replace scattered WhatsApp groups
- Supervision marketplace connects supervisees with supervisors

### Phase 4: Platform & Revenue (Months 18-36)
**Goal:** Sustainable revenue, clinic-tier expansion

- Clinic plans for group practices (multi-practitioner, shared clients)
- Razorpay-powered payment collection (MindStack takes processing fee margin)
- Premium features: advanced analytics, AI-assisted note summarization, insurance claim templates

## 6. Key Bets

### Bet 1: India-Specific Beats Global
Global tools like SimplePractice and TherapyNotes don't work for India. Different payment methods, different regulatory requirements, different professional hierarchies, different price sensitivity. A deeply localized tool will win over a translated global one.

### Bet 2: Bottom-Up Adoption
Enterprise sales to hospitals and clinics is slow and expensive. We bet on individual practitioners adopting MindStack for themselves, then bringing it to their practice. Bottom-up is faster, cheaper, and creates genuine product-market fit.

### Bet 3: Supervision is the Wedge, Not the Product
Supervision tracking gets us in the door, but the full practice management suite keeps practitioners. We must expand beyond supervision quickly to avoid being seen as a single-feature tool.

### Bet 4: Community Creates Retention
A practitioner might skip logging a note. They won't skip a peer discussion about a difficult case. Community engagement will be the strongest predictor of long-term retention.

---

## 7. What We Won't Do (For Now)

- **Teletherapy/video sessions** — Too many good tools exist (Zoom, Google Meet). Integration > building our own.
- **AI therapy bots** — Ethical minefield. We serve practitioners, not replace them.
- **Insurance billing** — India's mental health insurance landscape is nascent. We'll add this when the market is ready.
- **Global expansion** — India alone is a massive market. Focus beats breadth.
