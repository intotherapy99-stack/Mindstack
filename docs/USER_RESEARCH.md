# MindStack — User Research

## Research Methodology

- **20 semi-structured interviews** with mental health professionals across Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Chennai
- **5 client interviews** (therapy seekers aged 22-35)
- **Survey of 150 practitioners** via Instagram and WhatsApp professional groups
- **Shadowed 3 practitioners** for a full workday to observe workflow

## Key Research Insights

### Insight 1: The "Admin Tax" Is Real
**Finding:** Practitioners spend 4-6 hours per week on non-clinical admin (scheduling, payment tracking, note formatting, supervision logging). That's the equivalent of 3-4 lost client sessions — roughly 5,000-15,000 INR in lost income weekly.

**Implication:** MindStack's value proposition isn't "better software." It's "get 4 hours of your week back."

### Insight 2: Supervision Tracking Is Universally Broken
**Finding:** 18 out of 20 practitioners interviewed track supervision hours manually. 14 said they've had anxiety about whether their records would be accepted during professional reviews.

**Implication:** This is the emotional wedge. Supervision tracking isn't just a feature — it's relief from a persistent professional anxiety.

### Insight 3: Payment Conversations Are the #1 Discomfort
**Finding:** 16 out of 20 practitioners said collecting payments from therapy clients feels "ethically uncomfortable." Many delay sending reminders, leading to significant unpaid balances.

**Implication:** Automated, system-generated payment reminders remove the personal discomfort. The system sends the reminder, not the therapist. This is emotionally significant.

### Insight 4: Trust Is Non-Negotiable for Adoption
**Finding:** Practitioners will not use a tool that stores client data unless they trust it completely. "My clients trust me with their deepest secrets. I can't put that in some random app."

**Implication:** DPDP compliance, data encryption, and clear privacy messaging aren't features — they're prerequisites. We must lead with trust.

### Insight 5: Community Is a Retention Lever
**Finding:** Every practitioner is in 3-10 WhatsApp groups for professional discussion. But these groups are noisy, unmoderated, and hard to search. "I wish there was a better place to discuss cases."

**Implication:** A well-moderated community space within MindStack can drive daily engagement even when practitioners aren't seeing clients.

### Insight 6: Trainees Are the Growth Vector
**Finding:** India has approximately 5,000 mental health trainees at any given time. They have the most acute supervision tracking need and the highest tech comfort. They're also the most likely to recommend tools to peers.

**Implication:** Win trainees now, and you win established professionals in 3-5 years. The free tier should be optimized for trainee needs.

## Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

### Primary JTBD (Practitioner)
> **When** I finish a therapy session, **I want to** quickly document what happened and get on with my day, **so that** I have proper records without spending my evening doing admin.

### Secondary JTBDs
| Job | Current Solution | Pain Level |
|-----|-----------------|------------|
| Track which clients owe me money | Google Sheets / memory | High |
| Log supervision hours for review | Excel spreadsheet | Very High |
| Send session reminders to clients | Manual WhatsApp messages | Medium |
| Find a supervisor in my city | Ask colleagues / Instagram | High |
| Refer a client to a specialist | WhatsApp group message | Medium |
| Know if my practice is growing | Gut feeling | Medium |
| Give my client a booking link | Calendly (separate tool) | Low |
| Comply with data protection laws | "I don't really know how" | High (latent) |

## Survey Results (n=150)

### "What is your biggest practice management challenge?"
1. **Payment tracking & collection** — 67%
2. **Clinical documentation** — 54%
3. **Supervision hour tracking** — 51%
4. **Scheduling & reminders** — 43%
5. **Finding supervision / peer support** — 38%

### "Would you pay for a practice management tool?"
- Yes, if under 1,000 INR/month: **62%**
- Yes, at any price if it saves me time: **18%**
- No, I'd only use a free tool: **20%**

### "How do you currently track supervision hours?"
- Excel/Google Sheets: **58%**
- Paper logbook: **24%**
- Don't track regularly: **14%**
- Dedicated app: **4%**

## Design Implications

| Research Finding | Design Decision |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Practitioners feel awkward about payments | System-generated reminders with warm, professional tone |
| Trust is prerequisite | DPDP compliance badges visible, encryption messaging in onboarding |
| Supervision anxiety is real | Progress bar toward quarterly goals, celebrate milestones |
| Admin tax = lost income | Show "time saved" metrics in analytics dashboard |
| Trainees want to look professional | Public profile with professional layout regardless of experience level |
| Community is fragmented | Moderated spaces organized by topic, searchable archive |
| Diverse professional landscape | No "license number required" gates — inclusive onboarding for all roles |
